# Grade 3 Week 1: SHAMASH & THE CIRCLE - Artifact Requirements

## STEM History Components

### 1. Shamash Sun Disk Symbols and Ancient Mesopotamian Circular Art
- **Required:** Large drawing of Shamash's sun symbol with rays
- **Required:** Images of ancient Mesopotamian sun disk representations
- **Required:** Artistic representations of Shamash showing circular symbols
- **Function:** Help students understand the historical and cultural significance of circular symbolism in ancient Mesopotamia

### 2. Timeline Materials Showing Wheel and Circular Tool Development
- **Required:** Timeline pictures showing wheel development from 5,500 years ago to present
- **Required:** Examples of ancient pottery wheels, grinding wheels, and early transportation wheels
- **Function:** Demonstrate how circular innovations spread from Mesopotamia to world civilizations

### 3. Cross-Cultural Examples of Circular Design Spread
- **Required:** World map for marking innovation spread
- **Required:** Examples of circular tools and designs from Egypt, Europe, Asia
- **Function:** Show how Mesopotamian circular innovations influenced global technology development

### 4. Modern Examples Connecting to Ancient Circular Principles
- **Required:** Collection of modern circular objects with clear purposes
- **Required:** Before/after images showing circular improvements to designs
- **Function:** Help students connect ancient wisdom to contemporary applications

## Invention-Related Materials

### 1. Circle Construction and Measurement Tools Appropriate for Grade 3
- **Required:** Simple compasses or string-and-pencil setup
- **Required:** Rulers, string, paper circles for hands-on exploration
- **Required:** Protractors or angle guides for 45-degree divisions
- **Function:** Enable students to construct circles and discover geometric properties

### 2. Materials for Testing Circular vs. Non-Circular Structural Strength
- **Required:** Paper and tape for making tubes of different shapes
- **Required:** Books for weight testing structural strength
- **Required:** Various circular objects for measurement experiments
- **Function:** Allow students to test why circular structures are stronger

### 3. Layout Planning and Arrangement Supplies for Hands-On Activities
- **Required:** Small objects to represent desks and students for arrangement testing
- **Required:** Graph paper for layout planning
- **Required:** Measuring tools for distance calculations
- **Function:** Help students experiment with circular vs. other spatial arrangements

### 4. Examples of Circular vs. Angular Technology Comparisons
- **Required:** Various technological devices for circular component identification
- **Required:** Examples of tools showing circular design advantages
- **Function:** Demonstrate how engineers choose circular shapes for specific applications

## General Project Objects

### 1. Classroom Manipulatives for Circle Exploration and Measurement
- **Required:** Various sized circular objects (plates, lids, coins) for investigation
- **Required:** Non-standard measuring tools (paper clips, blocks, string)
- **Required:** Simple recording charts for pattern recognition
- **Function:** Provide hands-on materials for discovering circle properties and patterns

### 2. Art and Construction Supplies for Circular Design Challenges
- **Required:** Drawing paper and crayons for design activities
- **Required:** String, tape, markers for circle construction
- **Required:** Cardboard and tape for model construction
- **Function:** Enable students to design and test circular solutions to problems

### 3. Simple Calculation Tools and Problem-Solving Materials
- **Required:** Calculators for pattern recognition
- **Required:** Data recording sheets for measurement discoveries
- **Required:** Problem-solving strategy posters with pictures
- **Function:** Support mathematical exploration and pattern recognition

### 4. Innovation Project Construction Materials Suitable for Young Builders
- **Required:** Small building materials like blocks for testing
- **Required:** Space for students to make circles and test arrangements
- **Required:** Examples of circular playground equipment for inspiration
- **Function:** Allow students to create and test their own circular design innovations

## Total Artifacts Required: 24 items

## Storage and Organization Notes
- Most materials can be stored in labeled bins by activity type
- Measurement tools should be accessible for multiple activities throughout the lesson
- Art supplies need to be readily available for both individual and group work
- Technology examples should be displayed safely for observation but not student handling

## Safety Considerations
- All tools appropriate for Grade 3 use (ages 8-9)
- Sharp compasses should be teacher-supervised only
- Technology examples should be displayed for viewing only
- All testing materials should be safe for student manipulation

## Learning Objective Alignment
These artifacts support Grade 3 students in:
- **CCSS.3.G.A.1:** Understanding shape attributes through hands-on exploration
- **CCSS.3.G.A.2:** Partitioning shapes into equal areas using circle division
- **CCSS.3.MD.D.8:** Solving real-world perimeter problems with circular objects
- **NGSS.K-2-ETS1-1:** Gathering information about design problems through observation
- **NGSS.K-2-ETS1-2:** Creating models to show how object shape affects function
