### Right Triangle Properties - Mathematical Foundation
Examine the special properties that make right triangles the foundation of measurement science:

**Essential Properties:**
- **One 90° angle:** Exactly perpendicular - creates perfect vertical and horizontal reference
- **Pythagorean theorem:** a² + b² = c² (hypotenuse relationship) - never fails, always accurate
- **Complementary angles:** The two non-right angles always add to exactly 90°
- **Trigonometric ratios:** Sine, cosine, tangent relationships enable precise calculation
- **3-4-5 relationship:** Simple whole number example (3² + 4² = 5²) for practical construction

**Special Right Triangles:**
- **45-45-90 triangle:** Equal legs, hypotenuse = leg × √2

## B6: Mathematical Problem-Solving Workshop
**Duration:** 10-12 minutes  
**Learning Objective:** Students will solve multi-step problems involving right triangle precision, accuracy analysis, and measurement system design.

### Engineering Problem Set
**Problem 1: GPS Accuracy Optimization**
"GPS uses right triangle calculations with signals from 4 satellites to determine position. If satellite positions are known within ±2 meters and signal timing within ±5 nanoseconds, calculate maximum position error."

**Solution Process:**
1. Timing error distance: 5 × 10⁻⁹ seconds × 3 × 10⁸ m/s = 1.5 meters
2. Satellite position error: ±2 meters
3. Right triangle error propagation: √(1.5² + 2²) ≈ 2.5 meters per satellite
4. 4-satellite system geometric dilution: approximately 1.5× multiplier
5. Maximum position error: 2.5 × 1.5 ≈ 3.75 meters
6. Actual GPS accuracy typically better due to error correction algorithms

**Problem 2: Manufacturing Tolerance Analysis**
"A precision assembly requires parts to fit within 0.001 inch tolerance. Each part has corners that must be exactly 90°. Calculate maximum allowable angle error."

**Solution Process:**
1. At 1-inch distance from corner, tolerance = 0.001 inch
2. Angle error creates position error: distance × sin(angle error)
3. 0.001 = 1 × sin(angle error)
4. Angle error = arcsin(0.001) ≈ 0.057° ≈ 3.4 arcminutes
5. Manufacturing must control angle accuracy to better than 3 arcminutes
6. This requires precision tooling and measurement systems

**Problem 3: Surveying Error Propagation**
"A rectangular property survey starts with one perfect right angle and measures two sides: 500 feet and 750 feet, each ±0.1 feet accuracy. Calculate uncertainty in total property area."

**Solution Process:**
1. Nominal area: 500 × 750 = 375,000 square feet
2. Side measurement errors: ±0.1 feet each
3. Area error from side 1: 750 × 0.1 = ±75 sq ft
4. Area error from side 2: 500 × 0.1 = ±50 sq ft
5. Combined error: √(75² + 50²) ≈ ±90 sq ft
6. Area accuracy: 375,000 ± 90 sq ft (±0.024% accuracy)

- Accuracy requirements (how precise must measurements be?)
