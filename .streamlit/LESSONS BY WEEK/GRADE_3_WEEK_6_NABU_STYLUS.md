## B6: Math Problem-Solving Communication Adventures
**Duration:** 10-11 minutes  
**Learning Objective:** Students will solve age-appropriate problems involving information recording, symbol systems, and communication efficiency.

### Grade 3 Communication Problem Adventures
**Problem Set 1: Symbol Systems and Efficiency**

**Story Problem A: Message Length Calculation**
"Elena wants to write a message using 3 different symbols. Her message needs 5 symbols of type A, 8 symbols of type B, and 12 symbols of type C. How many total symbol marks will she need to make?"
- Calculate: 5 + 8 + 12 = 25 total symbols to create the complete message!

**Story Problem B: Information Storage**
"A clay tablet can hold 20 cuneiform symbols. If Marcus wants to record a message that needs 65 symbols, how many tablets will he need?"
- Work it out: 65 ÷ 20 = 3 tablets full, plus 5 symbols on a 4th tablet = 4 tablets total!

**Story Problem C: Communication Speed**
"Two students can each make 4 clear stylus marks per minute. Working together, how many marks can they create in 10 minutes?"
- Calculate: 4 + 4 = 8 marks per minute × 10 minutes = 80 total marks!

### Problem Set 2: Real-Life Applications
**Challenge Adventures:**

**Design Problem:** "Design an information sharing system for 6 different classroom subjects. If each subject needs 15 organized information cards, how many cards total will the system need?"
- Calculate: 6 subjects × 15 cards = 90 total information cards needed!

**Efficiency Problem:** "Compare two note-taking methods. Method A allows 10 words per minute with 95% accuracy. Method B allows 15 words per minute with 80% accuracy. Which method produces more accurate words per minute?"
- Calculate: Method A = 10 × 0.95 = 9.5 accurate words; Method B = 15 × 0.8 = 12 accurate words. Method B wins!

### Problem-Solving Helper Steps
**How to Solve Communication Problems:**
1. **Identify the information need:** What needs to be recorded, shared, or organized?
2. **Calculate requirements:** How much recording/storage capacity is needed?
3. **Consider efficiency:** What method produces the best results in reasonable time?
4. **Check for accuracy:** Does the solution preserve information reliably?
5. **Think about real use:** Would the system work for actual students and teachers?

### Real-Life Math Applications
**Where These Skills Help:**

- Understanding why different communication methods work better for different situations
- Calculating time and resources needed for information management projects
