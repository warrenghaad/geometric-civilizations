## A4: Geometric Analysis - 8-Point Star Properties
**Duration:** 8-10 minutes  
**Learning Objective:** Students will discover and construct the geometric properties that make 8-point stars effective for navigation and organization.

### Exploring 8-Point Stars
Using compasses, protractors, and grid paper, students will investigate:

**8-Point Star Parts to Discover:**
- **Center Point:** Where all 8 directions meet
- **Star Points:** The 8 tips that point in different directions  
- **Equal Angles:** Each direction is exactly 45 degrees from the next (360° ÷ 8 = 45°)
- **Equal Distance:** All star points are the same distance from the center

### Hands-On Construction
**Star Building Activity:**
1. **Draw the Base Circle:** Use a compass to make a circle
2. **Mark 8 Equal Points:** Divide the circle into 8 equal sections (45° each)
3. **Add the Star Points:** Draw lines from center to each of the 8 points
4. **Test Equal Spacing:** Measure to verify all angles are 45° and all distances are equal

**Problem-Solving Challenge:**
"You need to design a playground where 8 different activities are equally spaced around a central area. Use an 8-point star to plan where each activity should go so kids can easily move between them."

### Mathematical Discoveries
**What Students Find:**
- 8 equal divisions create the most practical number of directions for navigation
- More than 8 becomes confusing; fewer than 8 leaves gaps in coverage
- 45° angles are easy to estimate visually for navigation
- The pattern creates natural pathways between all areas

**Decomposition Activity:** Break down 8-point stars into their components:
- Central connection point (hub where everything meets)
- Eight radiating lines (paths or connections going outward)
- Equal angular spacing (fair distribution of directions)
- Consistent radius measurement (equal access to all directions)

**Artifacts Required:**
- Compasses and protractors for star construction
- Grid paper for measuring and verifying equal spacing
- Different colored pencils for highlighting the 8 directions

## B6: Mathematical Problem-Solving Workshop
**Duration:** 10-12 minutes  
**Learning Objective:** Students will solve multi-step problems involving 8-point star geometry, navigation calculations, and network efficiency.

### Grade 4 Star Pattern Problems
**Problem Set 1: Geometry and Measurement**

**Problem A: Star Construction**
"Draw an 8-point star in a circle with radius 6 cm. What is the angle between each star point?"
- Solution: 360° ÷ 8 points = 45° between each point

**Problem B: Navigation Distance**
"A ranger station is at the center of an 8-point star trail system. Each trail extends 4 miles from the center. How far is it from the end of the North trail to the end of the Northeast trail?"
- Solution: Use geometry to find that adjacent star points are about 3.1 miles apart

**Problem C: Hub Efficiency**
"A delivery truck starts at a central warehouse and must visit 8 stores located at the points of a star pattern. If each store is 12 miles from the warehouse, what's the shortest total distance to visit all stores and return?"
- Solution: 8 trips of 12 miles each = 8 × 12 × 2 = 192 miles (out and back to center)

### Problem Set 2: Network and System Analysis
**Challenge Problems:**

**Efficiency Problem:** "Compare two school bus systems: Linear (bus visits 8 schools in a line) vs. Hub (bus goes from central depot to each of 8 schools). If schools are 3 miles apart in the linear system and 5 miles from depot in the hub system, which uses less total driving?"

**Optimization Problem:** "Design an emergency response system for a town where fire stations must reach any location within 15 minutes. If fire trucks travel 30 mph, how should 3 fire stations be positioned using star pattern principles?"

### Problem-Solving Strategies
**Steps for Star Pattern Problems:**
1. **Draw the pattern:** Visualize the 8-point star or hub system
2. **Identify key measurements:** Angles, distances, or connections
3. **Use 8-point math:** Remember 360° ÷ 8 = 45° for angles
4. **Calculate systematically:** Work through each point or connection
5. **Check for efficiency:** Compare with other possible systems
