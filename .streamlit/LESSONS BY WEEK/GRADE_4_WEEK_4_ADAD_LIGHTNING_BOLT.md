## B6: Mathematical Problem-Solving Workshop
**Duration:** 10-12 minutes  
**Learning Objective:** Students will solve multi-step problems involving zigzag patterns, energy calculations, and wave system analysis.

### Grade 4 Zigzag Pattern Problems
**Problem Set 1: Geometry and Measurement**

**Problem A: Zigzag Path Length**
"A mountain road zigzags up a hill. If the road goes 100 feet forward and 50 feet up for each zigzag section, and there are 8 sections, how long is the total zigzag road?"
- Solution: Each section = √(100² + 50²) ≈ 112 feet; Total = 8 × 112 = 896 feet

**Problem B: Energy Absorption**
"A zigzag spring absorbs energy at each bend. If each of 6 bends absorbs 10 units of energy, how much total energy does the spring absorb? How does this compare to a straight spring that absorbs 45 units total?"
- Solution: Zigzag = 6 × 10 = 60 units; Zigzag absorbs 15 more units than straight spring

**Problem C: Wave Frequency**
"A zigzag signal makes 4 complete up-and-down cycles per second. How many cycles will it complete in 30 seconds?"
- Solution: 4 cycles/second × 30 seconds = 120 total cycles

### Problem Set 2: Engineering and Design Applications
**Challenge Problems:**

**Efficiency Problem:** "Compare two slide designs: straight slide (direct 15-foot drop) vs. zigzag slide (3 sections of 5 feet each). If the zigzag slide reduces speed by 30% compared to the straight slide, which is safer? Show your reasoning."

**Optimization Problem:** "Design a zigzag antenna that needs to fit in a 2-foot × 2-foot space but have a total wire length of 10 feet for better signal reception. How many zigzag sections do you need?"

### Problem-Solving Strategies
**Steps for Zigzag Problems:**
1. **Visualize the pattern:** Draw the zigzag to understand the geometry
2. **Break into sections:** Solve for one zigzag section first
3. **Use the Pythagorean theorem:** For diagonal zigzag measurements
4. **Multiply for total:** Scale up from one section to the full pattern
5. **Check for reasonableness:** Does the answer make sense for the real situation?
