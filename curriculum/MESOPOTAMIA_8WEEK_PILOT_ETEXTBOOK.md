# PROGRAM OVERVIEW

## The 8-Week Arc

This pilot program takes Grade 3 students on a journey through ancient Mesopotamia, discovering how geometric shapes weren't just mathematical concepts—they were sacred symbols, practical tools, and the building blocks of one of humanity's first great civilizations.

### Week-by-Week Summary

| Week | Deity/Theme | Element | Core Metaphor | System Application |
|------|-------------|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Shamash (Sun God) | Circle | Eternal justice, equal distance | Wheels, pulleys, potter's wheel |
| 2 | Sin (Moon God) | Crescent | Time cycles, monthly renewal | Lunar calendars, agriculture timing |
| 3 | Ishtar (Venus) | 8-Pointed Star | Divine guidance, navigation | Ishtar Gate, military banners |
| 4 | Inanna (Fertility) | Rosette | Life cycles, abundance | Royal seals, textile patterns |
| 5 | Temple Architecture | Square | Sacred boundaries, stability | Temple courtyards, city blocks |
| 6 | Babylon/Marduk | Rectangle | Order from chaos, planning | City grids, irrigation canals |
| 7 | Sacred Mountains | Triangle | Connection heaven-earth | Ziggurat profiles, structural stability |
| 8 | 3D Construction | Pyramid/Prism | Ascending to the gods | Volume, storage, monumental building |

# HOW TO USE THIS eTEXTBOOK

## For Teachers

This is your **Single Source of Truth (SSOT)**. Each week contains:
- Complete lesson narratives (read aloud or adapt)
- Image callouts with pedagogical rationale
- Hands-on activities with clear instructions
- Exit tickets for assessment
- Standards alignment

## For Students

Each week follows the same rhythm:
- **Day A**: Stories, art, and making things with our hands
- **Day B**: Science, math, and understanding how things work

## Image System

Images are coded as: `[IMAGE: CODE_description]`

Each image includes:
- **What it shows**: Description of the image content
- **Source**: Where to find it (museum, database)
- **Why this image**: Pedagogical purpose
- **Placement**: Where it appears in the lesson
