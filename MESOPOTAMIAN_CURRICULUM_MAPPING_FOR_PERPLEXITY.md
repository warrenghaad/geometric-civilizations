## MESOPOTAMIAN CURRICULUM CONTENT

### GEOGRAPHIC & CULTURAL FOUNDATION

**Region**: Ancient Mesopotamia (modern-day Iraq)  
**Rivers**: Tigris and Euphrates  
**Time Period**: 3500-539 BCE  
**Major Cities**: Ur, Uruk, Babylon, Nineveh  
**Cultures**: Sumerian, Babylonian, Assyrian  

**Key Images Needed**:
- Maps showing Tigris and Euphrates rivers in modern Iraq context
- Archaeological site photos of Ur, Uruk, Babylon ruins
- Mesopotamian irrigation canal systems and river agriculture
- Ancient city layouts showing geometric planning

### MESOPOTAMIAN DEITIES & GEOMETRIC CONNECTIONS

#### SHAMASH (Sun God)
**Geometric Elements**: Perfect circles, radial rays, solar disks  
**Mythology**: Daily circular journey across sky in fire chariot  
**Art Representations**: Sun disk symbols with 4 or 8 rays  
**Mathematical Concepts**: Circular motion, daily cycles, perfect circles  
**Artifacts**: Shamash relief carvings, Code of Hammurabi top section  

**Key Images Needed**:
- Shamash relief from Code of Hammurabi  
- Ancient Mesopotamian sun disk artifacts
- Archaeological sun god representations with geometric analysis
- Shamash symbols showing circular and radial geometry

#### SIN/NANNA (Moon God)  
**Geometric Elements**: Crescents, lunar cycles, circular phases  
**Mythology**: Travels in crescent-shaped boat, controls time cycles  
**Art Representations**: Crescent moon symbols, lunar deity carvings  
**Mathematical Concepts**: Cyclical patterns, lunar calendar mathematics  
**Artifacts**: Ur Ziggurat (dedicated to Sin), crescent moon ceremonial objects  

**Key Images Needed**:
- Ur Ziggurat architectural photos showing geometric construction
- Ancient Mesopotamian crescent moon artifacts
- Lunar calendar calculations on clay tablets
- Sin/Nanna deity representations with crescent geometry

### MESOPOTAMIAN ARTIFACTS WITH GEOMETRIC FOCUS

#### CYLINDER SEALS
**Geometric Elements**: Circular rolling motion, rosette patterns, repeated motifs  
**Function**: Personal signatures, business documents, property marking  
**Art**: Carved stone cylinders with intricate geometric patterns  
**Math Applications**: Pattern repetition, circular measurement, rotational designs  
**Examples**: Royal Cemetery of Ur seals, British Museum collection  

**Key Images Needed**:
- Cylinder seals and their clay impressions side-by-side
- Rosette patterns from cylinder seals with geometric analysis
- Step-by-step rolling process showing circular motion
- Close-up details of geometric patterns in seal carvings

#### MESOPOTAMIAN WHEELS
**Geometric Elements**: Perfect circles, spokes, rotational symmetry  
**Historical Significance**: First wheels in human history (c. 3500 BCE)  
**Types**: Solid wooden wheels, spoked wheels, potter's wheels  
**Math Applications**: Circumference, diameter, circular measurement  
**Archaeological Evidence**: Ur, Kish, and other Mesopotamian sites  

**Key Images Needed**:
- Mesopotamian solid wooden wheels archaeological finds
- Potter's wheels from archaeological excavations
- Ancient Mesopotamian chariots and carts with wheel details
- Geometric analysis of wheel construction and proportions

#### POTTERY & CERAMIC ART
**Geometric Elements**: Circular vessels, concentric circles, radial patterns  
**Techniques**: Potter's wheel usage, geometric decorative patterns  
**Examples**: Painted pottery with geometric designs, storage jars  
**Math Applications**: Circular construction, pattern repetition, symmetry  
**Sites**: Samarra culture pottery, Ubaid period ceramics  

**Key Images Needed**:
- Mesopotamian pottery showing concentric circle patterns
- Potter's wheel operations and circular vessel formation
- Geometric painted pottery designs with mathematical analysis
- Archaeological pottery fragments showing geometric decorations
