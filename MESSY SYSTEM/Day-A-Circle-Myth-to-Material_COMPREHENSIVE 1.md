### PART 1: OPENING MYTH (10-15 min)

#### The Story of Shamash: The Sun God's Circle

**Primary Myth:** The Daily Journey of Shamash

**Narrative Summary:**

Every morning, before the first rooster crows and before the first baker lights his oven, something magical happens in ancient Mesopotamia. The great sun god Shamash wakes up in his palace deep inside the eastern mountains. He puts on his golden crown—a perfect circle of light—and prepares for his most important job: bringing light, warmth, and justice to the world.

Shamash climbs into his chariot, pulled by magnificent horses made of fire. The great gates of the eastern mountains swing open, and Shamash begins his circular journey across the sky. As he travels, his circular sun disk shines down on everything below. Nothing can hide from Shamash's circular eye. He sees the honest farmer working in his field. He sees the dishonest merchant trying to cheat his customers. He sees children playing, mothers cooking, and scribes writing on clay tablets.

All day long, Shamash travels in his perfect circular path across the dome of the sky. And because he sees everything, the Mesopotamians knew that lies and cheating would always be discovered. Shamash's circular light reveals all truth.

When evening comes, Shamash reaches the western mountains. The gates open, and he descends into the underworld. But even at night, Shamash doesn't rest. He travels through the underworld, bringing light to the dead, judging souls, ensuring that justice exists even in the realm of shadows. And then, in the eastern mountains, he rises again, completing his eternal circle.

**Key Mythic Moments:**

1. **Shamash rises from the eastern mountains** - The circle of the sun emerges, bringing order to the darkness of night
2. **Shamash sees all from his circular vantage point** - No secret can escape the all-seeing circular eye
3. **Shamash travels his circular path** - The sun's daily journey represents cosmic order and regularity
4. **Shamash descends to judge the underworld** - Even in death, the circular path of justice continues

**Geometric Focus:** The **circle** represents cosmic order, eternal cycles, all-seeing justice, and the completeness of divine power. Shamash's circular sun disk has no beginning and no end—it represents something that lasts forever and sees everything.

### PART 2: GEOMETRIC METAPHOR EXTRACTION (5-10 min)

#### What the Myth Establishes

**Primary Metaphor:**

> **CIRCLE = Eternal Order, All-Seeing Justice, Completeness**

**Supporting Metaphors:**

- **Circle = Cycles**: Day and night, life and death, seasons—everything moves in circles
- **Circle = Wholeness**: A circle is complete, perfect, with no breaks or gaps
- **Circle = Watchfulness**: Shamash's circular sun disk is like an eye that sees everywhere at once

**Ontological Position:**

- **Relation to other elements**: The circle is the first and most important shape—it's the sun, the most powerful thing in the sky
- **Cosmic role**: Divine and eternal—circles represent the gods and their endless power
- **Human relationship**: Humans can observe circles but not control them—we can't make the sun stop or change its path

**Visual/Semiotic Code:**

| Mythic Element | Geometric Form | Semiotic Meaning | Cultural Value |
|----------------|----------------|------------------|----------------|
| Shamash's sun disk | Perfect circle | All-seeing justice | "Truth always emerges" |
| Daily sun journey | Circular path | Eternal cycles | "Order is reliable" |
| Horizon to horizon | Complete arc | Cosmic completeness | "Divine power is whole" |
| No beginning/end | Endless curve | Eternal existence | "Gods are immortal" |

### PART 3: GEOMETRIC METAPHOR IN MYTHIC ART (10-15 min)

#### How Artists Depicted the Myth Using Geometry

**Artifact Analysis 1: Cylinder Seal with Solar Disk**

**Type:** Cylinder Seal (rolled to make impressions in clay)
**Date:** Neo-Assyrian Period (c. 911-612 BCE)
**Mythic Scene:** Shamash enthroned with sun disk rays
**Geometric Encoding:** The sun is depicted as a perfect circle with straight lines radiating outward in all directions
**Semiotic Function:** When merchants and officials rolled this seal, they invoked Shamash's watchful eye over their transactions—"Let the all-seeing sun witness this contract is honest"

**Artifact Analysis 2: Kudurru (Boundary Stone)**

**Type:** Stone monument marking property boundaries
**Date:** Kassite Period (c. 1595-1155 BCE)
**Mythic Scene:** Multiple divine symbols, with Shamash's sun disk prominently displayed
**Geometric Encoding:** The sun disk appears as a circle with four straight rays pointing to the four directions (north, south, east, west) and four wavy rays between them
**Semiotic Function:** The circle on the boundary stone meant "Shamash watches this boundary—moving it would be a crime against the sun god"

**Artifact Analysis 3: Temple Relief Carvings**

**Type:** Stone relief from temple walls
**Date:** Old Babylonian Period (c. 2000-1600 BCE)
**Mythic Scene:** Shamash rising from mountains or descending into underworld
**Geometric Encoding:** Mountains form triangular peaks, while Shamash's disk is always perfectly circular, emphasizing the contrast between earthly (triangular) and divine (circular) realms
**Semiotic Function:** Temple visitors saw this daily, reinforcing the message: "The circular sun represents eternal divine order rising above our earthly triangular mountains"

**Pattern Recognition:**

> "Notice across all these artifacts: the circle consistently appears as the sun disk, always associated with Shamash, always representing divine justice and eternal order. This is not coincidence—this is systematic visual encoding of mythic truth."

### PART 4: GEOMETRIC ELEMENT INSTITUTIONALIZED IN MATERIAL CULTURE (25-35 min)

#### The Translation: From Sacred Myth to Everyday Object

**Mechanism:**

```
MYTH defines meaning (Shamash = circular sun = justice)
↓
ART depicts myth using geometry (sun disk = circle)
↓
SACRED OBJECTS adopt same geometry (temple seals, boundary stones)
↓
EVERYDAY OBJECTS replicate geometry (pottery, jewelry, tools)
↓
UBIQUITOUS EXPOSURE creates automatic association (circle = divine order)
```

| Architectural Feature | Geometric Form | Mythic Connection | Daily Message |
|-----------------------|----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Temple courtyard | Circular sacred space | Shamash's circular domain | "Sacred space mirrors heaven" |
| Sun disk carved above gates | Circular relief | Shamash watches all who enter | "Justice sees you" |
| Circular windows (rare) | Round openings | Light of Shamash enters | "Divine light illuminates" |
| Rosette decorations | Circular flower patterns | Ishtar's fertile circles | "Life radiates from center" |

**Daily Encounter Context:**

- **When Seen:** Entering temple for festivals, passing city gates, viewing palace facades
- **Subliminal Message:** "Circular divine order watches over our square human order"
- **Behavioral Reinforcement:** "Act justly—the circular eye of Shamash sees all"

**Ritual Use:**

### PART 6: THE ARCHITECTURAL BRIDGE (5-10 min)

**Transition to Day B:**

**Case Study:** The Ziggurat's Circular Elements

**Why Architecture Bridges Form & Function:**

**FORM (Day A):**
- Circular decorative elements (rosettes on walls) carry mythic meaning: divine beauty, Ishtar's fertility
- Circular courtyard communicates: enclosed sacred space mirroring heavenly realm
- Reinforces social order through: "Those inside the circular sacred space have special access to gods"

**FUNCTION (Day B):**
- Same circular architectural elements solve practical problems: water drainage (circular pipes), structural support (circular columns)
- Engineering principles employed: circular arches distribute weight evenly
- Mathematical relationships utilized: circumference calculations for building circular features

**The Integration:**

> "The **circle** in the ziggurat's decorative rosettes is simultaneously:
> - A mythic symbol connecting to Ishtar (FORM)
> - An optimal geometric shape for decorative repetition (FUNCTION)
>
> This dual nature is what makes geometric elements powerful thinking tools—they encode both meaning AND utility."

**Preview Tomorrow:**

"Tomorrow we'll see how the circle that Mesopotamians internalized through Shamash's myth and daily material culture enabled them to solve complex mathematical, architectural, and engineering problems. The shape that carried cosmic meaning also carries structural genius. The potter's wheel, the wheeled cart, the water-lifting wheel—all depend on the circle's unique geometric properties. The circle that meant 'divine justice' also meant 'smooth continuous motion.' Both meanings are true. Both meanings matter."

## Day A Wrap-Up

**Key Takeaways:**

1. **Circle** emerged from Shamash's myth as metaphor for eternal order, all-seeing justice, and divine completeness
2. Artists encoded this metaphor in cylinder seals, boundary stones, and temple reliefs using perfect circular sun disks
3. Material culture replicated the geometry in jewelry, pottery, seals, grinding stones, and architectural decorations
4. Daily exposure made **circle = divine order/justice/cycles** automatic and internalized
5. This internalization prepared people to use **circle** as a thinking tool—recognizing its geometric properties tomorrow

**Reflection Questions for Students (Age-Appropriate):**

- How many circles did you see today before coming to class?
- Why do you think ancient people chose a circle for the sun instead of a square or triangle?
- If you were a Mesopotamian child wearing a circular bracelet, how might that make you feel protected?
- Can you think of objects in your own home that are circular? Why do you think they're made that way?

## Teaching Notes

**Timing:**
- Myth: 10-15 min (adjust based on student engagement)
- Metaphor extraction: 5-10 min (use visual aids)
- Mythic art: 10-15 min (show images, ask questions)
- Material culture: 25-35 min (MAJORITY OF LECTURE—show many examples)
- Embodied cognition: 10-15 min (make it interactive—have students count circles they can see)
- Architectural bridge: 5-10 min (preview tomorrow's excitement)

**Emphasis:**
- Spend MOST time on material culture section (Part 4)
- Show MANY images—visual saturation is key for Grade 3 students
- Make connections explicit: "This pottery bowl has the same circular shape as Shamash's sun disk"
- Use call-and-response: "What shape is the sun?" → Students: "Circle!"
- Build to architectural bridge carefully—it's pedagogical pivot to Day B

**Grade 3 Adaptations:**
- Use simpler vocabulary (avoid "ontological," say "how things connect to gods")
- Add more interactive elements (counting circles, drawing circles, moving in circles)
- Keep myth story narrative and engaging—use dramatic voice
- Relate to students' lives: "Just like you see the sun rise every morning..."
- Break up lecture with movement: "Let's all stand and make a big circle with our arms!"

**Materials Needed:**
- Images of cylinder seals with sun disk
- Images of kudurrus with divine symbols
- Images of pottery with circular decorations
- Images of jewelry (bracelets, earrings)
- Images of ziggurats showing circular rosettes
- Drawing materials for students to sketch circles
- Optional: Real circular objects (plate, bracelet, coin) to pass around

**Assessment Opportunities:**
- Can students identify circles in ancient artifacts?
- Can students explain why Shamash was shown as a circle?
- Can students name 3-5 everyday objects that were circular in ancient Mesopotamia?
- Can students articulate the connection between myth (Shamash) and material culture (circular objects)?
