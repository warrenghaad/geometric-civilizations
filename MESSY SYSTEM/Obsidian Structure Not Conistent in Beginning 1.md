Circle: The Efficiency Paradox {#circle}
What it means:

Infinite rotational symmetry = no "weak points" directionally
Maximum area for minimum perimeter (nature's efficiency hack)
Distributes stress evenly in all directions
Engineering Reality:

Wheels, bearings, pipes, domes — all exploit this uniform stress distribution
A circular column can bear load from ANY direction equally
A square column? Weak along the diagonals
Pressure vessels (tanks, submarines, boilers) are cylindrical because stress distributes uniformly—no stress concentration points
Design Implication:
When you need omnidirectional strength or minimum material for maximum capacity, go circular. It's why manhole covers are round (won't fall through), why gears mesh in circles, why your eye is a sphere.

Triangle: The Structural Rebel {#triangle}
What it means:

Inherent rigidity = once you fix the three side lengths, the shape is locked
A square can be pushed into a parallelogram (shear it); a triangle cannot
Engineering Reality:

Trusses, bridges, roof frames — triangles are the skeleton of load-bearing structures
The Eiffel Tower? Triangles everywhere
Triangulation is literally the process of adding diagonals to make other polygons rigid
A rectangle needs diagonal bracing; a triangle doesn't
Design Implication:
When you need structural integrity with minimal material, triangulate. This is why:

Bicycle frames use triangular geometry
Roof trusses are triangular
Geodesic domes (Buckminster Fuller) tessellate triangles/hexagons
Medieval wooden buildings used triangular bracing (why they still stand 800 years later)
Square: The Measurement Tyrant {#square}
What it means:

Right angles = perpendicularity = the basis of coordinate systems
Perfect tessellation = tiles with zero gaps or overlaps
Creates the grid—the fundamental organizational system
Engineering Reality:

All measurement systems are based on right angles (Cartesian coordinates, blueprints, CAD software)
Bricks, tiles, floor plans, circuit boards — all square/rectangular
Standardization depends on the square: you can mass-produce identical units and they fit together perfectly
This is why cities are gridded (easier to navigate, easier to build, easier to measure)
Design Implication:
When you need standardization, modularity, and measurability, use squares/rectangles. This is the logic behind:

Urban planning (Manhattan grid)
Modular housing
Pixel-based digital design
Assembly-line manufacturing

The Design Thinking Angle
These three shapes encode three different problem-solving strategies:

Circle = "How do I distribute forces evenly?" (Efficiency)
Triangle = "How do I maximize rigidity with minimal material?" (Strength)
Square = "How do I standardize and organize?" (Order)
Every complex engineering problem is essentially asking: Which of these three atomic strategies do I need, and in what combination?

A bridge needs triangular rigidity (trusses) + circular efficiency (bearings, joints) + square modularity (standard beam sizes).

This is how they see that inventions aren't magic—they're combinations of these three atomic shapes solving specific problems.

Great question. These three live in the same neighborhood but have different jobs. Think of a record collection:

Tagging = sticky notes on album covers
Metadata = the liner notes printed on the jacket

Here’s the clean breakdown.

Tagging
What it is: Lightweight labels you attach to items for quick filtering and grouping.
Purpose: Fast retrieval, ad-hoc organization, flexible views.
Properties:
Flat or lightly hierarchical (#gelement/circle, #civ/mesopotamia)
Many-to-one and many-to-many
Easy to change; low governance
When to use:
You want to find “everything with triangles” or “all Mesopotamia lessons for Grade 4”
You’re exploring and don’t want to commit to strict structure yet
In Obsidian terms: hash tags; backlinks can complement tags.
Metadata
What it is: Structured fields that describe an item’s attributes.
Purpose: Precision, automation, querying, dashboards.
Properties:
Key-value pairs (YAML frontmatter): title, era: uruk, magic.m: 0.8
Machine-readable and consistent
Powers Dataview queries, charts, pipelines
When to use:
You need sortable/filterable tables (e.g., list all artifacts where semiotics.primary_metaphor includes “sun” and era includes “neo-babylonian”)
You want to compute things (MAGIC vectors, counts, dates)
In Obsidian terms: the frontmatter block at the top; also inline fields if you prefer.
Ontology
What it is: The formal conceptual model that defines entities, their types, properties, and relationships.
Purpose: Shared meaning at scale; prevents chaos; enables interoperability.
Properties:
Defines classes (Myth, GeometricElement, Artifact, MaterialObject, Lesson)
Defines relationships (depicts, derives_from, institutionalizes, teaches, appears_in)
Can be expressed as a schema or graph (RDF/OWL in the enterprise world; plain docs + conventions in Obsidian)
When to use:
You want consistency across thousands of notes and collaborators
You need to answer relationship questions: “Which myths define Circle, which artifacts visualize that, and which material objects institutionalize it in the Neo-Assyrian era?”
In Obsidian terms: your documented model + enforced templates + linting/checklists. You don’t need OWL to benefit.

Quick cheat sheet
Core design principle: verbs are atomic and directional

Atomic: each verb says exactly one thing. No synonyms. No compound meanings.
Directional: always record in the same direction (e.g., Myth defines GeometricElement; don’t flip it).
Evidence chain: Source anchors truth claims; downstream entities reference Sources minimally and consistently.
evidences: Source → Artifact | MaterialObject | Myth | Concept
Meaning: “This Source provides grounds to assert the existence/attributes/interpretation of the target entity.”
Use when:
A publication, catalog, excavation report, museum entry, image, or scholarly article supports factual claims.
Scope of evidence:
Existence: proves the entity is real (artifact recorded in a museum database).
Attributes: materials, dates, sites, iconography details.
Interpretations: myth identification, function, conceptual reading (with confidence levels).
Fields:
In Source: evidences: ["art-bm-123","myth-shamash","concept-triangulation"]
In target entity: sources: ["src-2025-bm-catalog","src-2011-porada"]
Edge cases:
Conflicting sources: allow multiple evidences with notes: “interpretation contested.”
Image-only evidence: acceptable for existence; add a status note: “awaiting scholarly corroboration.”

G3-1A: Shamash & The Circle

Myth snapshot:
Shamash/Utu, sun god, patron of justice. Kings present themselves receiving “right measures” from Shamash; light = fairness.
Icon: solar disk with rays; seated Shamash on mountain motifs on seals and stele.
Material objects:
Cylinder seals with solar disks and ray rosettes; kudurru boundary stones bearing Shamash symbol; round offering stands; circular grinding stones; potter’s wheel as cultural technology.
Common provenances: Old Babylonian seals; Neo-Babylonian boundary stones; museum holdings often label Shamash by a solar disk above a scene.
Geometric technique:
Circle made by fixed center and constant radius. In practice: cord-and-peg or compass-like dividers; potter’s wheel yields true circular profiles.
Math/system application:
Sexagesimal division of the circle aligns with 6, 12, 24, 60 partitions. Equal arcs correspond to equal time spans; circularity ensures consistent angular measures.
Comptroller/standards:
Temple/palace workshops establish measures; scribal tablets list reciprocals and metrology (time/angle congruence). Seals with Shamash used to authenticate legal acts—symbol + measure as authority.
“But for” causation:
Without stable circle-making and equal divisions, synchronized timekeeping and fair apportionment falter; courts and labor schedules can’t scale reliably.

import sqlite3
import yaml
import os
import re
from pathlib import Path

DB_PATH = "sda_curriculum.db"
OBSIDIAN_VAULT = Path("~/Documents/Obsidian - Main Vault/02_DATABASE").expanduser()

def parse_md(file_path):
    with open(file_path, 'r') as f:
        content = f.read()
    # Extract YAML frontmatter
    match = re.match(r'^---\n(.*?)\n---', content, re.DOTALL)
    if not match:
        return None, content
    frontmatter = yaml.safe_load(match.group(1))
    body = content[match.end():]
    return frontmatter, body

def extract_concept_links(body):
    # Find [[Concepts/...]] links
    return re.findall(r'\[\[Concepts/(.*?)\]\]', body)

def upsert_artifact(conn, frontmatter, obsidian_path, concept_names, tags):
    cursor = conn.cursor()
    # Insert or update artifact
    cursor.execute("""
        INSERT INTO artifacts (title, type, period, week_number, day, section_number, grades,
                               ontology_stage, form_category, function_category, formula,
                               dashboard_input_status, review_status, source_pdf, museum,
                               catalog_number, date_range, summary, obsidian_path, updated_at)
        VALUES (?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, CURRENT_TIMESTAMP)
        ON CONFLICT(obsidian_path) DO UPDATE SET
            title=excluded.title, type=excluded.type, period=excluded.period,
            week_number=excluded.week_number, day=excluded.day, section_number=excluded.section_number,
            grades=excluded.grades, ontology_stage=excluded.ontology_stage,
            form_category=excluded.form_category, function_category=excluded.function_category,
            formula=excluded.formula, dashboard_input_status=excluded.dashboard_input_status,
            review_status=excluded.review_status, source_pdf=excluded.source_pdf,
            museum=excluded.museum, catalog_number=excluded.catalog_number,
            date_range=excluded.date_range, summary=excluded.summary, updated_at=CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
    """, (
        frontmatter.get('title'), frontmatter.get('type'), frontmatter.get('period'),
        frontmatter.get('week_number'), frontmatter.get('day'), frontmatter.get('section_number'),
        str(frontmatter.get('grades')), frontmatter.get('ontology_stage'),
        frontmatter.get('form_category'), frontmatter.get('function_category'),
        frontmatter.get('formula'), frontmatter.get('dashboard_input_status'),
        frontmatter.get('review_status'), frontmatter.get('source_pdf'),
        frontmatter.get('museum'), frontmatter.get('catalog_number'),
        frontmatter.get('date_range'), frontmatter.get('summary'), obsidian_path
    ))
    artifact_id = cursor.lastrowid or cursor.execute("SELECT id FROM artifacts WHERE obsidian_path=?", (obsidian_path,)).fetchone()[0]
    
    # Link concepts
    for concept_name in concept_names:
        cursor.execute("INSERT OR IGNORE INTO concepts (name, category) VALUES (?, ?)", (concept_name, 'unknown'))
        concept_id = cursor.execute("SELECT id FROM concepts WHERE name=?", (concept_name,)).fetchone()[0]
        cursor.execute("INSERT OR IGNORE INTO artifact_concepts (artifact_id, concept_id) VALUES (?, ?)", (artifact_id, concept_id))
    
    # Link tags
    for tag in tags:
        cursor.execute("INSERT OR IGNORE INTO tags (tag) VALUES (?)", (tag,))
        tag_id = cursor.execute("SELECT id FROM tags WHERE tag=?", (tag,)).fetchone()[0]
        cursor.execute("INSERT OR IGNORE INTO artifact_tags (artifact_id, tag_id) VALUES (?, ?)", (artifact_id, tag_id))
    
    conn.commit()

def sync_obsidian_to_sqlite():
    conn = sqlite3.connect(DB_PATH)
    for md_file in OBSIDIAN_VAULT.rglob("*.md"):
        frontmatter, body = parse_md(md_file)
        if not frontmatter:
            continue
        concept_names = extract_concept_links(body)
        tags = frontmatter.get('tags', [])
        obsidian_path = str(md_file.relative_to(OBSIDIAN_VAULT.parent))
        upsert_artifact(conn, frontmatter, obsidian_path, concept_names, tags)
    conn.close()
    print("Sync complete.")

if __name__ == "__main__":
    sync_obsidian_to_sqlite()

const admin = require('firebase-admin');
const vision = require('@google-cloud/vision');
const { Storage } = require('@google-cloud/storage');

admin.initializeApp();
const visionClient = new vision.ImageAnnotatorClient();
const storage = new Storage();

exports.extractTextFromPDF = functions.storage.object().onFinalize(async (object) => {
  const filePath = object.name;
  if (!filePath.endsWith('.pdf')) return;

  const bucketName = object.bucket;
  const gcsUri = `gs://${bucketName}/${filePath}`;

  // Call Vision API
  const [result] = await visionClient.documentTextDetection(gcsUri);
  const fullText = result.fullTextAnnotation.text;

  // Write to Firestore
  const docId = filePath.replace(/\//g, '_').replace('.pdf', '');
  await admin.firestore().collection('pdfs').doc(docId).set({
    filename: filePath.split('/').pop(),
    storage_path: filePath,
    uploaded_at: admin.firestore.FieldValue.serverTimestamp(),
    ocr_status: 'completed',
    extracted_text: fullText,
    annotations: {},
    synced_to_local: false
  }, { merge: true });

  console.log(`OCR completed for ${filePath}`);
});

from firebase_admin import credentials, firestore

cred = credentials.Certificate("path/to/serviceAccountKey.json")

SQLITE_DB = "sda_curriculum.db"
OBSIDIAN_VAULT = Path("~/Documents/Obsidian - Main Vault/02_DATABASE/Artifacts").expanduser()

def sync_firebase_to_local():
    conn = sqlite3.connect(SQLITE_DB)
    cursor = conn.cursor()
    
    # Query Firestore for unsynced, ready annotations
    docs = db.collection('pdfs').where('synced_to_local', '==', False).where('annotations.dashboard_input_status', '==', 'ready').stream()
    
    for doc in docs:
        data = doc.to_dict()
        annotations = data.get('annotations', {})
        
        # Upsert to SQLite
        cursor.execute("""
            INSERT INTO artifacts (title, type, period, week_number, day, section_number, grades,
                                   ontology_stage, form_category, function_category, formula,
                                   dashboard_input_status, review_status, source_pdf, summary, obsidian_path)
            VALUES (?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?, ?)
            ON CONFLICT(obsidian_path) DO UPDATE SET
                title=excluded.title, type=excluded.type, period=excluded.period,
                week_number=excluded.week_number, day=excluded.day, section_number=excluded.section_number,
                grades=excluded.grades, ontology_stage=excluded.ontology_stage,
                form_category=excluded.form_category, function_category=excluded.function_category,
                formula=excluded.formula, dashboard_input_status=excluded.dashboard_input_status,
                review_status=excluded.review_status, source_pdf=excluded.source_pdf, summary=excluded.summary
        """, (
            annotations.get('title'), annotations.get('type'), annotations.get('period'),
            annotations.get('week_number'), annotations.get('day'), annotations.get('section_number'),
            str(annotations.get('grades')), annotations.get('ontology_stage'),
            annotations.get('form_category'), annotations.get('function_category'),
            annotations.get('formula'), annotations.get('dashboard_input_status'),
            annotations.get('review_status'), data.get('filename'), data.get('extracted_text', '')[:200],
            f"02_DATABASE/Artifacts/{annotations.get('title', 'UNKNOWN').replace(' ', '_')}.md"
        ))
        
        # Generate Obsidian MD
        obsidian_file = OBSIDIAN_VAULT / f"{annotations.get('title', 'UNKNOWN').replace(' ', '_')}.md"
        with open(obsidian_file, 'w') as f:
            f.write("---\n")
            for key, value in annotations.items():
                f.write(f"{key}: {value}\n")
            f.write("---\n\n")
            f.write(f"Summary: {data.get('extracted_text', '')[:300]}\n\n")
            f.write(f"Source PDF: {data.get('filename')}\n")
        
        # Mark as synced in Firestore
        db.collection('pdfs').document(doc.id).update({'synced_to_local': True, 'synced_at': firestore.SERVER_TIMESTAMP})
        print(f"Synced {annotations.get('title')}")
    
    conn.commit()
    conn.close()

if __name__ == "__main__":
