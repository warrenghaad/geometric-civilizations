# LESSON 1: SHAMASH & THE CIRCLE OF JUSTICE

**Shape**: Circle  
**Deity**: Shamash (Sun God, Justice)  
**Duration**: 50 minutes  
**Images**: [99][100][80]

## MYTH (5 min): The Sun Disk of Equal Justice

**Scene 1**: Shamash rises at dawn holding perfect golden sun disk. Eight cities surround Babylon, each needing equal justice.

**Scene 2**: Demon Pazuzu tilts Shamash's chariot - some cities get too much light, others darkness. Inequality and chaos reign.

**Scene 3**: Shamash discovers only a perfect circle—where every point is equidistant from center—ensures fairness reaches all equally. He calculates: 360° ÷ 8 cities = 45° spacing.

**Scene 4**: Aerial view of eight cities bathed in equal golden light. Justice restored through geometric perfection.

## ARTIFACTS (10 min):
1. **Shamash Tablet (Sippar Stele)** - British Museum ME 91000, ~870 BCE, 29×18cm, sun disk 14cm diameter
2. **Bronze Sun Standard** - British Museum ME 122200, ~2500 BCE, 56cm diameter, 16-fold radial symmetry
3. **Cylinder Seal with Solar Deity** - Met Museum 41.160.192, 1800-1600 BCE, concentric circles
4. **Pottery Sun Disk** - Penn Museum B16228, impressed circles
5. **Palace Relief Fragment** - Yale BC 16841, carved sun disk

## GEOMETRIC LAB (15 min):
**Layer 1**: Identify circular patterns (sun disk, concentric rings, radial rays)
**Layer 2**: Measure diameter/radius, calculate circumference C=2πr
**Layer 3**: Decompose into circles + radiating lines
**Layer 4**: Measure angles between rays (360°÷n)
**Layer 5**: Verify rotational symmetry, test reflection lines

## DESIGN ACTIVITY (10 min):
Create personal sun disk with:
- **Grade 3**: Template with 8 rays, color symmetrically
- **Grade 4**: Construct using compass, calculate 45° spacing
- **Grade 5**: Design custom n-ray system, prove symmetry order

## INNOVATIONS (3 min):
- Pottery wheel (continuous rotation)
- Cylinder seals (rolling circles)
- Circular city walls (defensive geometry)
- Water wheels (irrigation)
- Astronomical observations (circular sky dome)

## ASSESSMENT (2 min):
- **G3**: Count rays, identify circles, locate center point
- **G4**: Calculate circumference given radius, measure angles
- **G5**: Prove C/d=π through measurement of 3 circles
