### What is a Civilisation?

A civilisation is an advanced state of human society. 
There are seven characteristics that define a civilisation.  These include:

A Stable Food Supply
System of Government
Social Structure
Religious System
Written Language
Advances in Technology
Advances in Art

### Why were River Valleys Important?

River valleys were important for: 
Farming - large amounts of people could be fed from crops grown.
Trade - goods and ideas could move from place to place. 
Development of Cities – cities grew up in these valleys and became the centres of civilisations.

## Slide 8

**Layout:** Title and Content

**Content:**

### Irrigation

In the spring, the rivers often flooded, leaving behind rich soil for farming. 
However, flooding was very unpredictable. It might flood one year, but not the next. Therefore, every year, farmers worried about their crops.
Over time, the farmers learned to build dams and channels to control the seasonal floods. They also built waterways, and ditches to bring water from the rivers to their fields. 
This way of watering crops is called irrigation.

### Food Supply

The Sumerians grew several kinds of crops. 
The most valuable one was barley, which was used to make flour and bread. 
Dates were also very valuable to the Sumerians. They were eaten once ripe or else they were dried for future consumption. Dates also made an excellent wine.
