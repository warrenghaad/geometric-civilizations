Program Description (SDA: Material Culture Through Ritual) SDA is a K–8 integrated curriculum that teaches students to see geometric elements as both cultural signs (Day A) and functional systems (Day B). Each week anchors a single geometric element inside a civilization’s myth and material culture, then traces how embodied ritual practice leads to mathematical insight, scientific capability, and invention. Using the MAGIC framework (Math, Aesthetics, Geometric Element, Ideology/Myth, Comptroller), students move from story and objects to structure and causation: myth narrates an element as metaphor; rituals internalize its form; material culture encodes it into daily life; math reveals its properties; engineering uses those properties to solve problems; institutions scaffold and standardize the outcomes.

Weekly Thesis By interacting with myth, geometric cultural elements (patterns, tessellations), and material objects through ritual, students internalize the geometric element’s design and functional properties. This embodied cognition enables recognition of its mathematical truths and scientific applications. When transformed (rotated, reflected, scaled, tessellated, extruded), the element yields inventions and, in combination with other elements, compound systems. Ideology inspires; institutions (Comptroller) enable or constrain; discoveries, innovations, and inventions crystallize.
