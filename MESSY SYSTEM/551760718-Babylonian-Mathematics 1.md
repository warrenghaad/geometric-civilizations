### Babylonian mathematics

Also know as Assyro-Babylonia Mathematics.
Developed or practice by the people of Mesopotamia, from days of the early Sumerians to fall of Babylon in 539 BC.
The Babylonians used base number system is 60. the base number system used today for measuring time, 60 seconds in a minute, 60 mins in an hour and for measuring 360 degrees in a full turn.
It is range of numeric and more advanced mathematical practice in the ancient near east, written in cuneiform script.
Babylonian mathematics is derived from some 400 clay tablets unearthed since the 1850s.
