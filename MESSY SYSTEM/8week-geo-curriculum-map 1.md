# 8-Week Geometry, Myth, and Innovation Curriculum Mapping

---

## Structure
- **Weeks 1–8**: Each week features a focused geometric element/pattern (circle, triangle, square, spiral, radial, grid, polygon/star, fractal/complexity)
- **2 lessons per week:**
    - **Lesson 1 (Day A):** Visual semiotics, ritual, myth/narrative, material culture, drawing/art practice of geometric element (FORM, meaning, culture)
    - **Lesson 2 (Day B):** Internalized element equips design thinking; its math/science, discoveries, innovations, inventions (FUNCTION, problem-solving)

## Overview Table (Weeks 1–8)
| Week | Element/Pattern    | Day A (FORM/myth/art/culture)                                     | Day B (FUNCTION/design science/math/stem)                        |
|------|--------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1    | Circle             | Circle in creation myth, divine cycles, offerings, art drawing    | Math of circle: constants, astronomy; wheels, timekeeping        |
| 2    | Triangle           | Sacred mountain, mediation, ziggurat, art triangle drawing        | Measurement, surveying, triangle math, rigidity; grid survey     |
| 3    | Square             | Tablet of Destinies, city walls, justice, layout; square drawing  | Area/volume, right angles, square grids, data & planning         |
| 4    | Spiral             | Cosmic growth/chaos/order, ritual spiral, art & spiral practice   | Golden ratio, screw, ramps, coil-building, irrigation, pottery   |
| 5    | Radial Symmetry    | Sun/stars, solar deities, radial altars, radial design drawing    | Radial math, symmetry, rotational tech, wheels/networks          |
| 6    | Polygon/Star       | Stars as fate, poly-star art, textiles, star diagram drawing      | Tiling, polygons in structure/design, multi-pointed innovation   |
| 7    | Grid/Tessellation  | Land division, ritual space, patterned art, grid drawing/art      | Tiling theory, logic gates, city/grid planning, modularity       |
| 8    | Fractal/Complexity | Trees, rivers, lightning myths, recursive patterns, complexity art| Self-similarity, scaling, networks, coding, complexity in tech   |

## Week Template (expand for each element)

### Week N: [Element/Pattern]

#### Lesson 1 (Day A): FORM
- Core myth or ritual (narrative ready for class)
- Artistic examples (artifact, design history, image description)
- Visual semiotics (how/where it signals cosmic/cultural meaning)
- Material culture (objects, rituals, spaces, gestures)
- Art/embodiment practice (draw, model, perform element)

#### Lesson 2 (Day B): FUNCTION
- Mathematical qualities/geometry
- Design/engineering challenge or demo
- Historical discoveries or inventions (how understanding the element changed tech or science)
- Model-making or drawing reinforcing concept
- Innovation: how element reframed design thinking

## Sample Week (Week 1: Circle)

### Lesson 1
- **Myth:** Creation with circular chaos; offerings round; Sun/Moon gods
- **Artifact:** Cylinder seals with circular motifs; circular altars
- **Visual semiotics:** Circle = eternity, unity, cycle
- **Ritual/material:** Round vessels, circular dances/processions
- **Art practice:** Draw/paint circle using compasses, body tracing

### Lesson 2
- **Math:** Pi, circumference, area, round numbers, celestial calculations
- **Design:** Water clock, wheels, gears, rotating systems
- **Invention:** Wheel, pottery wheel, round shields, timekeeping
- **Innovation:** Circle as problem-solving tool (rotating motion, measuring)
- **Model:** Cardboard sundial/clock project

## Generalizable Weekly Planning Prompt

"For Week X: [Element/Pattern], generate two lessons:
1. Lesson 1: Myth, ritual, visual semiotics, art history, material/object/cultural practice, and a creative drawing or embodied practice reinforcing the element.
2. Lesson 2: How internalizing the element empowered design thinking; its mathematical and scientific role; discoveries, innovations, and hands-on or drawing/design invention project showing the element's novel application.
For each lesson: list all primary, secondary, and tertiary learning outcomes, at least three artifacts/phenomena/examples for myth/culture and for STEM/innovation; suggest at least one hands-on/model/drawing task that assesses understanding through making/doing."
