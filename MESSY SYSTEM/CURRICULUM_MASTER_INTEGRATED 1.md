## Curriculum Overview

### Core Philosophy

**Pedagogical Model:** Myth → Material Culture → STEM Innovation

**Learning Arc:**
```
Day A (FORM): Mythological narrative establishes geometric element as metaphor
              ↓
              Mythic art depicts the metaphor using geometry
              ↓
              Material culture embeds geometry in daily life
              ↓
              Embodied cognition internalizes meaning through ubiquitous exposure
              ↓
              Architectural bridge transitions to Day B

Day B (FUNCTION): Mathematical principles of geometric element
                  ↓
                  Engineering applications and innovations
                  ↓
                  Design thinking and problem-solving
                  ↓
                  Modern legacy and contemporary applications
                  ↓
                  Synthesis: Form + Function integration
```

### Geometric Elements Progression

| Week | Element | Mythic Association | Functional Application |
|------|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Circle | Shamash (sun god) = eternal order, divine cycles | Rotation, wheels, potter's wheel, circular motion |
| 2 | Triangle | Sacred mountain = hierarchy, ascent to heaven | Rigidity, structural strength, 3-4-5 right angles |
| 3 | Square/Rectangle | Four Winds = earthly order, boundaries | Area calculation, grid systems, modular building |
| 4 | Polygons (8-pointed star) | Ishtar/Venus = love, war, cosmic cycles | Rotational symmetry, tessellation, pattern design |
| 5 | Composite Shapes | Lamassu = divine protection, hybrid forms | Complex geometric composition, design synthesis |
| 6 | Patterns | Ishtar Gate = repeating order | Tessellation, mathematical patterns, visual rhythm |
| 7 | Cosmic Integration | All deities and shapes integrated | Sacred geometry, complete cosmology |
| 8 | Final Project | Student synthesis | Design thinking, creative application |

#modern-legacy #practical-application #technology
```

**Key Concepts:**
1. **Mathematical Properties**
   - Circumference = π × diameter (or 2πr)
   - Rotational symmetry (infinite)
   - All points equidistant from center
   - Area = πr²

2. **Technological Inventions**
   - Potter's Wheel: Rotational symmetry → smooth spinning → symmetric pottery
   - Wheeled Cart: Rolling motion → reduced friction → easy transportation
   - Water-Lifting Wheel: Continuous rotation → constant work → efficient irrigation

3. **Modern Applications**
   - Industrial pottery and ceramics
   - All wheeled vehicles (cars, bikes, trains)
   - Hydroelectric turbines and wind power
   - Rotating machinery

#structural-engineering #roof-truss #ziggurat-engineering #triangulation
#surveying #strongest-shape
```

**Key Concepts:**
1. **Mathematical Properties**
   - Rigidity (only polygon that cannot deform)
   - Angle sum always = 180°
   - Pythagorean theorem (a² + b² = c²)
   - 3-4-5 triangle creates perfect 90° angle

2. **Technological Innovations**
   - Ziggurat: Triangular profile distributes weight to stable base
   - Roof Trusses: Triangular bracing creates strong lightweight structures
   - Surveying: 3-4-5 rope method creates perfect right angles
   - Triangulation: Measuring distances indirectly

3. **Modern Applications**
   - All bridge trusses (Warren truss, Pratt truss)
   - Roof structures in every building
   - Crane booms and tower structures
   - GPS triangulation technology

**Key Concepts:**

   - Rectangular Containers: Perfect tessellation, no wasted space

3. **Modern Applications**
   - All modern buildings (rectangular rooms)
   - City grid planning (Manhattan, Chicago, Beijing)
   - Agricultural field divisions
   - Modular construction and manufacturing
   - Paper, books, screens (all rectangular)

## Assessment Framework

### Formative Assessment (Ongoing)

**Day A (Myth to Material):**
- Can students identify geometric element in ancient artifacts?
- Can students explain mythic significance of shape?
- Can students name 3-5 material culture objects containing the shape?
- Can students connect myth to daily life encounters?

**Day B (STEM Innovation):**
- Can students explain mathematical properties of shape?
- Can students describe how shape solves engineering problems?
- Can students identify modern applications of ancient innovation?
- Can students apply geometric thinking to new problems?

### Summative Assessment (End of Week)

**Week Assessment Components:**
1. **Geometric Recognition:** Identify shape in various contexts (20%)
2. **Myth Comprehension:** Explain mythic narrative and meaning (20%)
3. **Mathematical Understanding:** Demonstrate knowledge of properties (20%)
4. **Application:** Apply concepts to solve problems (20%)
5. **Synthesis:** Integrate form and function perspectives (20%)

### Portfolio Assessment (End of Unit)

**Final Project Rubric:**
- Research and Planning (25%)
- Geometric Accuracy (25%)
- Creative Integration (25%)
- Presentation and Explanation (25%)

## Implementation Guide

### Teacher Preparation (Per Week)

**Before Week Begins:**
1. Read both Day A and Day B comprehensive lesson plans
2. Review PowerPoint slides and speaker notes
3. Gather or prepare image assets for projection
4. Print student worksheets
5. Prepare hands-on materials (blocks, rope, paper, rulers, etc.)
6. Set up classroom for physical activities

**Day A Setup:**
- Images ready for each slide (use extracted PPT images)
- Physical geometric shapes for students to touch
- Materials for embodied cognition activities
- Drawing/coloring materials

**Day B Setup:**
- Demonstration materials (as specified in lesson plan)
- Measurement tools (rulers, string, etc.)
- Building materials (blocks, craft sticks, etc.)
- Calculators (if needed for older grades)

### Pacing Guide

**50-Minute Version (Core Content):**
- Follow timing in comprehensive lesson plans
- Focus on main concepts, skip some examples
- Prioritize hands-on activities over extended explanations

**75-Minute Version (Extended):**
- Include all content from comprehensive lesson plans
- Add more examples and student discussion
- Deeper exploration of material culture
- Extended hands-on activities

### Differentiation Strategies

**For Advanced Learners:**
- Additional mathematical challenges
- Research extension projects
- More complex geometric constructions
- Leadership roles in group activities

**For Struggling Learners:**
- Simplified vocabulary
- More visual aids and physical manipulatives
- Peer support partnerships
- Focus on concrete examples before abstract concepts

**For English Language Learners:**
- Visual glossaries of geometric terms
- Physical demonstrations with minimal language
- Bilingual resources where available
- Picture-based assessments
