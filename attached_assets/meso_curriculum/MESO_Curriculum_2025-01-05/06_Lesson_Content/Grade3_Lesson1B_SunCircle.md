### Section 4 — The Myth (Read-Aloud, ~3 min)

1. The sun wakes over the river, a bright round face that comes back each day in a steady way.¹
2. In the town, children gather around a round loaf on a round plate.²
3. So many hands reach at once that the loaf looks smaller just from the hurry.³
4. The sun's light makes a soft ring on the ground, like a quiet line that goes all the way around.⁴
5. "Make a circle," says an old kind voice the children feel more than hear.⁵
6. They step back, hold hands, and make a round.⁶
7. The smallest child walks to the middle and sets the loaf in the center.⁷
8. "Half and half," she says, and pulls a string straight across.⁸
9. Two parts wait, each the same, and everyone breathes.⁹
10. The sun moves a little—same distance, same pace.¹⁰
11. "Four fair shares," the voice whispers, and the string crosses the middle again.¹¹
12. Now there are four parts, just right for four friends, and no one is left out.¹²
13. Crumbs travel around the circle, one by one, until the plate is light and clean.¹³
14. The sun stands high and round. "Keep the circle," says the voice, "and it will keep you."¹⁴

## 3) Teacher Script (Minute-by-Minute)

### Objectives
1. Make a circle; find center
2. Partition into halves/fourths
3. Explain why equal parts support fair sharing

### Materials
- Pre-cut paper circles
- Glue sticks, crayons, pencils
- Brads + paper clips (spinners)
- Printed worksheets and templates
- Projector and doc cam

### Flow

**0:00–2:00 S01 SEL**
- Say: "When we share, what helps it feel fair?"
- Do: Trace a big circle in the air.

**2:00–2:30 S02 Hook**
- Say: "Listen for the round idea in this story."

**2:30–6:00 S03–S10 Myth Read-Aloud**
- Say: Read one sentence per slide.
- Ask: T01 "Show me half." T02 "Show me four."
- Manage: Calm voice; slow pace; add RM if needed.

**6:00–11:00 S11–S15 Mini-Lesson**
- Model: Center dot; one line → halves; two lines crossing → fourths.
- Check: "Point to the center." "Do the lines cross the middle?"

**11:00–16:00 S16–S20 Notice & Wonder**
- Ask: "Where is the round?" "What is equal here?"

**16:00–26:00 S21–S25 Decomposition (doc cam)**
- Do: Draw and label circle, center, halves, fourths.
- Fix: If lines miss center, redraw passing through dot.

**26:00–36:00 S26–S31 Art Activity — Circle Collage**
- Steps: Fold once → halves; fold again → fourths; glue into frames.
- Support: Dotted centers available; peer check equal parts.

**36:00–44:00 S32–S36 Math & Life**
- Do: Build 2- and 4-section wheels; practice "next turn."
- Language: "Equal parts make it fair."

**44:00–50:00 S37–S40 Build + Exit**
- Do: Assemble spinner with brad; test with peers.
- Exit: "How did the circle help us be fair today?"

### Differentiation

**ELL:** Frames—"It is fair because parts are equal."  
**Extra support:** Pre-dotted centers; ruler/straight edge  
**Extension:** Why thirds aren't halves/fourths; design 3-friend plan

### Assessment

**Evidence:** Collage accuracy, spinner correctness, exit sentence  
**Rubric:** See Section 6

### Classroom Management

**Materials stations:** roles: Runner, Glue Lead, Cleanup  
**Safety:** Brads handled at a single station with adult oversight

## 4) Student Worksheet (3 Print Pages)

**Header:** Name ____ Date ____ Class ____

### Page 1 — Make Equal Parts

1. Draw a circle. Put a dot in the center.
2. Draw one straight line across the center to make two equal parts (halves).
3. Draw a second line across the center, crossing the first, to make four equal parts (fourths).
4. Color opposite parts the same color to check equality.

### Page 2 — Fair Sharing

A) Two friends: Shade each half a different color.  
B) Four friends: Shade each fourth a different color.  
C) Why is going through the center fair? Write: "The parts are equal because ____."

### Page 3 — Everyday Math

- **Snack time:** Draw a round cookie and show fourths.
- **Job wheel:** Color who goes next on a 4-section wheel.
- **Reflection:** "Today a circle helped me be fair when _____."

**Teacher Answer Cues:** Lines pass through the center; regions equal by area; opposite colors match.
