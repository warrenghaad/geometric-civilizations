Your dimensional model, precisely defined
- Dimension 1: Knowledge within a single concept family
  - 1a Atomic unit
    - Example: 5×1 = 5; a single symmetry rule; one SEL skill name; one symbol–meaning link.
  - 1b Molecular unit (the whole “line” within-domain)
    - Example: Mastery of the entire fives table (5×n), skip-counting by 5s; executing a single geometric rule across all placements; applying one SEL strategy reliably across the same type of situations.
  - Note: Only Dimension 1 has subparts 1a and 1b, as you require.

- Dimension 2: How (cross-application)
  - Definition: Applying the concept (atom or molecule) across other concepts within or adjacent to the domain, including multi-step procedures, word problems, constraint coordination, or mixing families.
  - Examples:
    - Math: Use 5×n with other tables, decompositions, or arrays; solve word problems combining 5×n with place value or ratio structures.
    - Art/geometry: Combine one transformation rule with another (rotation + reflection; rotation + color inversion); integrate perspective with proportional spacing.
    - SEL: Combine one regulation strategy with planning, opposite action, or attention routines.
  - Key: How can attach to either the atom (How does 5×1 work in a situation?) or the molecule (How do the fives apply with 3×n, arrays, or word problems?).

- Dimension 3: Why/Why not (systems reasoning, rationale, trade-offs)
  - Preconditions: Sufficient coverage of How so that the learner understands how things are built and combined.
  - Definition: Justify choices among multiple Hows; analyze trade-offs, efficiency, and purpose-fit.
  - Examples: Why choose 5×n via 10s-and-halves; why rotational symmetry over reflection to express balance; why this SEL strategy here and not another.

- Dimension 4: What if (transfer across domains, time, or constraints)
  - Definition: Move the system into novel contexts, counterfactuals, resource limits, or cross-domain fusion; forecast and adapt.
  - Examples: Apply 5×n rhythmically in music or budgeting; redesign a tessellation without a compass; transfer a knot continuity principle to an Oceanic navigation chart; build a future plan using calendar cycles.

How to implement this exactly in your K–8 curriculum
Journal/page layout per task
- Top row:
  - D1a What: one atom only (single fact/rule/symbol/skill).
  - D1b Line: demonstrate the entire family within-domain (e.g., 5×n across several n; one symmetry rule applied around all placements).
- Middle row:
  - D2 How: apply the atom or the molecule to other structures (mix tables/rules; combine constraints; word problems).
- Bottom row:
  - D3 Why/Why not: written or oral rationale with trade-offs.
  - D4 What if: a transfer/adaptation scenario and a brief plan.
